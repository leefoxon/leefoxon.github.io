1

The "Confidence Paradox" ‚Äî surface confidence masks deep operational anxiety

97% feel broadly confident, yet 78% say management is more complex than before, 69% say it takes too much of their time, and 73% say better support would reduce their stress. These two truths coexist throughout the data ‚Äî and this tension is Citation's commercial entry point.

97% broadly confident 78% experiencing increased complexity 73% want support to reduce stress
2

Payroll complexity and errors are systemic, not isolated

42% have discovered a payroll error or underpayment. 67% say Fair Work Awards or pay rules take more time than they should. 49% find payroll management unnecessarily complex ‚Äî rising to 54% in mid-sized businesses. This is a widespread, recurring pain point across every cohort.

67% Fair Work friction 42% discovered payroll errors 49% say payroll is unnecessarily complex
3

Psychosocial risk is the fastest-emerging gap in the compliance landscape

While physical H&S confidence is high (89%), confidence managing psychosocial risks falls to 77% ‚Äî and only 55% find people management rewarding and not stressful. 30% are concerned about a psychosocial issue escalating, and this is now a direct regulatory requirement. It sits at the frontier of compliance for most small-to-mid businesses.

89% confident on physical H&S 77% confident on psychosocial ‚Äî the widest gap 30% worried about psychosocial escalation
4

Skills shortages and data security are the top near-term concerns

Skills shortages top the concern list at 38%, followed closely by data breaches (37%), missing a compliance requirement (33%), and workplace safety incidents (34%). These are not hypothetical fears ‚Äî they are live operational pressures that create clear entry points for structured risk conversations.

38% ‚Äî Skills shortages 37% ‚Äî Data breach risk 34% ‚Äî Workplace injury risk
5

Mid-sized businesses (20‚Äì100 staff) carry the highest strain ‚Äî with the highest appetite for support

This cohort scores highest on payroll complexity (54%), time burden, and pay error worry (48%). They are too large for informal owner-managed compliance, too small for dedicated compliance teams. 77% say right support would reduce stress, and 34% want a single integrated HR/payroll/compliance system ‚Äî the strongest demand of any segment.

54% find payroll unnecessarily complex 77% say right support reduces stress 34% want integrated system
6

System fragmentation is universal ‚Äî single integrated solutions are the top desire

Only 19% manage people, payroll and safety through a single system. 55% use 2‚Äì3 systems, 22% use 4 or more. The top service wished for today is a single integrated HR/payroll/compliance system (30%), followed by an online learning platform (29%). Only 12% say they need none of the services listed.

81% using 2+ separate systems 30% want single integrated platform 88% have at least one unmet service need
7

The regulatory treadmill is the defining forward-looking pressure

Looking ahead, talent attraction (37%) and evolving compliance (35%) top future challenges. Three of the top five future concerns involve regulatory burden. 78% feel prepared for future changes ‚Äî but only 57% find law changes manageable, suggesting preparedness confidence may be more aspiration than reality.

37% ‚Äî Attracting & retaining talent 35% ‚Äî Evolving compliance requirements 31% ‚Äî Keeping up with new laws
